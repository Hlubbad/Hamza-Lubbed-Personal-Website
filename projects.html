Project Title: Logic Gates Construction

Project Description

The team designed, constructed and explained the NOT-gate, AND-gate and OR-gate circuits using discrete components such as transistors and resistors on a solderless breadboard. Implementations used common NPN transistors, resistors for biasing and pull-ups/pull-downs, and LEDs as output indicators to verify truth-table behaviour. The goal was to demonstrate how basic digital logic is realised in hardware and to observe practical considerations like wiring, signal levels and propagation effects.

My Contribution

I was a member of Team 3 and contributed approximately 25% of the work, including breadboard assembly, testing and documentation.

Project 2 ‚Äî Hour of Code Community Outreach

Event: CS Education Week 2025

Project Title: Introducing AI and Coding to Children

Project Description

As part of Team 3, I participated in a community service event to introduce foundational Computer Science and AI concepts to local children. We delivered presentations on ADA University and the mechanics of AI before leading a hands-on coding workshop. Using the "Bug Arena" game on Microsoft MakeCode Arcade, we taught students how to construct and refine block-based algorithms to control characters in a battle arena.

My Contribution

As a member of Team 3, I managed the technical implementation and software demonstrations for the workshop. Because of the language barrier, I focused my contribution on:

  • Technical Mentorship: Guiding children through the Microsoft MakeCode block-based logic, using visual coding to bridge the language gap.
  • Algorithm Optimization: Assisting students in refining their 'Bug' movements by explaining mathematical concepts like rotation angles and speed variables visually.
  • Software Management: Ensuring the 'Bug Arena' game and development environment were properly configured and troubleshooting technical issues during the battle levels.
  • Event Documentation: Managing the certification process and recording the workshop progress to evaluate the algorithm's effectiveness.
